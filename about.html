The Story Behind the Voice

Ten years, hundreds of stages, one voice.

Before the ballrooms and the galas, there was a girl singing harmonies in a church choir loft. This is how that girl became the voice your guests will remember.

Sydney Small — soundcheck before a gala evening

Where It Began

Raised on Gospel. Refined by Jazz. Fluent in Everything Between.

"I didn't choose music so much as music kept choosing me — at every turn, on every stage, it was the thing that made a room feel alive."

I grew up on the second pew, watching my grandmother direct the choir every Sunday. By eight, I was singing solos. By fifteen, I was arranging harmonies. Gospel taught me the most important lesson of my career: a song isn't finished until the room feels it.

In my early twenties I fell hard for jazz — the phrasing of Ella Fitzgerald, the ache of Etta James, the ease of Nat King Cole. I spent years in lounge residencies learning how to hold a room with nothing but a melody and a microphone. Pop and modern soul came naturally after that, because underneath every great song is the same thing: an honest story, told well.

Today, that blend — gospel warmth, jazz discipline, pop instinct — is what I bring to every wedding, gala, and private event I sing. It's why my sets never feel like background music. They feel like the reason people came.

— Sydney Small

The Journey

A Decade in the Making

Every performer has a path. Mine runs from choir lofts to conservatory classrooms to more than two hundred private events — and it's still unfolding.

The Early Years

First Solos, First Standing Ovation

Raised in a musical family, I sang my first choir solo at eight years old. Church became my first stage, my first vocal coach, and my first lesson in singing for people rather than at them.

2011 – 2015

Formal Training & the Jazz Awakening

I studied vocal performance with a concentration in jazz studies, training in classical technique, breath control, sight-reading, and improvisation. My professors gave me discipline; late nights at open-mic jazz sessions gave me nerve.

2015 – 2018

Residencies & the Lounge Circuit

Weekly residencies at supper clubs and hotel lounges — four sets a night, three nights a week. Hundreds of hours reading rooms, taking requests on the fly, and building a repertoire that now spans over 300 songs.

2018 – 2021

The Turn to Private Events

A bride heard me sing at a lounge and asked me to perform her first dance. That one wedding became ten, then fifty. I discovered the work I love most: singing for the biggest moments of people's lives.

2021 – Today

200+ Events and Counting

Weddings, black-tie galas, corporate evenings, anniversaries, and proposals across the region. Ten years on stage, five-star rated by clients, and still treating every booking like the only one on the calendar.
